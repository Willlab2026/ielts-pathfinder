1.3 Understanding the Types of Information

📚 Chapter 1: Understanding Task 1 3 Exercises
In IELTS Writing Task 1, information can be shown in different forms, such as a diagram, a map, a pie chart, a table, a bar chart, or a graph.

Before learning how to write Task 1 answers, you should first become familiar with these common types of visual information. By completing the following exercises, you will develop a better understanding of them.

Exercise 3
Read each task description and look at the visual. Identify the type of information shown. Write the letter of the correct type in the box.
Types:   A. bar chart   B. table   C. pie chart   D. map   E. diagram   F. graph
Question 1
The proportion of different categories of families living in poverty in Australia in 1999.
Type:
Answer: B. table — The data is presented in rows and columns showing family types and their poverty proportions.
Question 2
The different levels of post-school qualifications in Australia and the proportion of men and women who held them in 1999.
Type:
Answer: A. bar chart — The visual uses horizontal bars to compare male and female qualification levels.
Question 3
The proportion of the population aged 65 and over between 1940 and 2040 in three different countries.
Type:
Answer: F. graph — The visual shows three line curves plotted over time (1940–2040), which is a line graph.
Question 4
A new supermarket (S) is planned for the town and there are two possible sites for the supermarket.
Type:
Answer: D. map — The visual shows a town layout with roads, buildings and possible sites for the supermarket.
Question 5
Information about water use worldwide and water consumption in two different countries.
Type:
Answer: F + B (graph + table) — This question combines a line graph (global water use trends) and a table (water consumption in Brazil and Congo). Task 1 sometimes uses two visuals together.
Question 6
The life cycle of the silkworm and the stages in the production of silk cloth.
Type:
Answer: E. diagram — The visual shows a process / cycle diagram illustrating stages rather than numerical data.
Question 7
Units of electricity production by fuel source in Australia and France in 1980 and 2000.
Type:
Answer: C. pie chart — The visuals show four pie charts comparing fuel source proportions in Australia and France across two years.
